About Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium

Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium is a multi-sports stadium located in New Delhi, India. Named after the first Prime Minister of India, the stadium is primarily a venue for football and athletics. With a seating capacity of 60,254, it is the fourth largest stadium in India, 27th largest in Asia, and 103rd largest in the world.

The stadium was designed and constructed to meet the international standards set by FIFA, the Asian Football Confederation (AFC), and World Athletics (WA). Originally built to host the athletic events and ceremonies of the 1982 Asian Games, it has also hosted the 1989 Asian Championships in Athletics and remains a key venue for sporting and cultural events.
